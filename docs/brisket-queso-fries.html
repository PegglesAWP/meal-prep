Ingredients

Brisket

  • 900g (32oz) flat cut brisket or lean steak of choice
  • 400g (14oz) can crushed fireroasted tomatoes
  • 120g (½ cup) red enchilada sauce
  • 200g (7oz can) chipotle peppers in adobo
  • 80g (3 Tbsp) green chiles
  • 60g (3 Tbsp) honey
  • 1Tbsp salt
  • 2tsp onion powder
  • 2tsp black pepper

Blended Queso Sauce

  • 800g (3 ⅓ cups) 2% cottage cheese
  • 100g (4 slices) American cheese
  • 150g (1 ½ cups) extra sharp cheddar cheese
  • 80g (⅓ cup) red enchilada sauce Salt & pepper to taste
  • 5g (1 tsp) nutritional yeast Chopped cilantro (optional)

Fries

  • 160g (1 ⅓ cups) frozen fries per portion added flavor.

Instructions

  1. Trim the flat cut brisket by removing all excess separable fat (reference trimming guide if needed). Once trimmed, place the meat in the slow cooker. Add the fire-roasted tomatoes, red enchilada sauce, chipotle peppers, green chiles, honey, salt, onion powder, and black pepper. Stir to coat the beef evenly. Cover and cook on high for 4–5 hours or low for 5–6 hours, until the beef is tender.
  2. While the beef cooks, prepare the blended queso sauce. Shred the American cheese and cheddar cheese. Combine in a blender with the 2% cottage cheese, red enchilada sauce, nutritional yeast (if using), and salt and pepper. Blend until smooth. If the mixture is too thick to blend, lightly heat the ingredients in a pan or microwave until warm (do not overheat), then blend again. Set aside.
  3. Once the beef is fully cooked, remove from the slow cooker and shred or chop into bite-sized pieces. Return the beef to the slow cooker and mix with the cooking juices. Pour the blended queso sauce over the beef in the slow cooker. Stir to combine, ensuring the beef is evenly coated. Optionally, top with chopped cilantro for
  4. Store brisket queso in 10 separate serving containers. For the fries, I recommend cooking fresh each time you have this recipe while storing the brisket queso on its own. To reheat, microwave for 2-3 minutes, mix, then microwave another 1-2 minutes to finish warming up (don’t overheat)
