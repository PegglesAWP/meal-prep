Ingredients

Beef

  • 1340g (48 oz) 93% ground beef

Chili Base

  • 3 yellow onions
  • 1cloves garlic
  • 30g (2 Tbsp) butter
  • 45g (3 Tbsp) tomato paste
  • 480g (2 cups) beef bone broth
  • 480g (2 cups) red enchilada sauce
  • 45g (3 Tbsp) apple cider vinegar
  • 794g (28oz) can of fire roasted diced tomatoes
  • 200g (7oz can) chipotle peppers
  • 425g (15oz) black beans
  • 20g (1 Tbsp) honey

Spice Blend

  • 1Tbsp each: salt, ancho chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder
  • 2tsp each: smoked paprika, cumin, oregano
  • 1tsp each: black pepper, cayenne
  • 0.125tsp cinnamon

Toppings & Garnish

  • 400g (1 ¾ cups) 0% plain skyr
  • 100g (⅓ cup) 1/3 fat cream cheese
  • 200g (1 ¾ cups) sharp cheddar cheese Handful of cilantro containers.

Instructions

  1. Dice the yellow onions. Cook in a hot pan over high heat until translucent and turning brown. Lower heat to medium, add minced garlic, butter and tomato paste. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Transfer the onion, garlic, and tomato paste mixture to the slow cooker with the ground beef. Add beef bone broth, red enchilada sauce, apple cider vinegar, fire roasted diced tomatoes, chipotles, black beans, honey, salt, ancho chili powder, garlic powder onion powder, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, black pepper, cayenne, and cinnamon. Mix well.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 4-5 hours or high for 3-4 hours, until the beef is tender and fully cooked. Once done, break up the meat with a wooden spoon, mixing until evenly combined. Remove from heat, grate the cheddar, and stir in softened 1/3 fat cream cheese and cheddar until melted and creamy.
  4. Divide into 8 servings and serve the chili in bowls topped with a dollop of 0% plain skyr and fresh chopped cilantro for added flavor. For leftovers, let the chili cool completely. Store in freezer-safe
