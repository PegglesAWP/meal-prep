Ingredients

Meat

  • 1790g (64oz) boneless, skinless chicken thighs

Red Curry Peanut Sauce

  • 120g (½ Cup) peanut butter
  • 100g (⅓ Cup) Thai red curry paste
  • 30g (2 Tbsp) soy sauce
  • 30g (2 Tbsp) fish sauce
  • 50g (2 ½ Tbsp) honey
  • 15g (1 Tbsp) grated ginger
  • 15g (1 Tbsp) grated garlic Juice + zest of 1 lime
  • 160g (⅔ cup) coconut cream freezer-safe containers for meal prep.
  • Toppings: Juice of 1-2 limes (to taste) Chopped Thai basil (handful)

Rice Bowls

  • 640g (3 ¼ cups) short-grain rice (dry weight)
  • 1150g (4 ¾ cups) chicken bone broth

Instructions

  1. Grate the ginger and garlic. Mix in a medium bowl with peanut butter, Thai red curry paste, soy sauce, fish sauce, honey, lime juice, zest, and coconut cream until smooth. Divide the sauce in half.
  2. Place chicken thighs in the slow cooker and pour half the sauce over them. Mix to coat evenly. Cover and cook on high for 3-4 hours or low for 4-5 hours, until tender.
  3. Combine rice and chicken bone broth in a pot, bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the liquid is absorbed. Let sit covered for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork. Based on type of rice being used, adjust liquid content.
  4. Once the chicken is done, shred lightly. Add the remaining sauce and juice of 1-2 limes (to taste). Stir well, add chopped Thai basil and let sit for 5-10 minutes to absorb flavors.
