Ingredients

Creamy Sauce

  • 480g (1 ¾ cups) 0% Greek yogurt
  • 240ml (1 cup) 1% milk
  • 20g (4 tsp) Dijon mustard Chives, chopped, for garnish

Cornstarch Slurry

  • 1Tbsp cornstarch
  • 1Tbsp cold water

Beef and Marinade

  • 1790g (64oz) steak of choice
  • 10 grated garlic cloves
  • 45g (3 Tbsp) butter
  • 2 yellow onions, diced
  • 300g (3 cups) frozen mixed mushrooms
  • 907g (4 cups) beef bone broth
  • 60g (4 Tbsp) Dijon mustard Salt, pepper, and onion powder to taste

Pasta

  • 900g (32oz) orzo

Instructions

  1. Cube the beef into bite-sized pieces and set aside. Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Dice the onions and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Grate the garlic and stir into the skillet with the butter, and frozen mixed mushrooms. Cook for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms release their liquid and it evaporates, leaving the mixture fragrant and browned.
  2. Transfer the onion and mushroom mixture to the slow cooker. Add the cubed beef, beef bone broth, Dijon mustard, and season with salt, pepper, and onion powder to taste. Stir to combine. Cover and cook on high for 3–4 hours or low for 4–5 hours, until the beef is tender.
  3. While the beef cooks, partially boil the orzo for 2 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  4. Once the beef is tender, add the partially boiled orzo to the slow cooker along with the 0% Greek yogurt,
  5. If the mixture is too thin, thicken with a cornstarch slurry by mixing the cornstarch and water, gradually adding it to the slow cooker. Stir until the desired consistency is reached.
  6. Divide into 12 servings, garnish with more chives if desired. Store leftovers in the freezer.
