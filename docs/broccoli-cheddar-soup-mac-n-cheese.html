Chicken

  • 1340g (48oz) boneless, skinless chicken thighs

Spices

  • 2 yellow onions
  • 30g (2 Tbsp) minced garlic
  • 30g (2 Tbsp) Worcestershire
  • 20g (4 tsp) Dijon mustard
  • 1Tbsp each: onion powder, garlic powder, salt
  • 2tsp black pepper
  • 2tsp smoked paprika

Broccoli

  • 400g (4 cups) Broccoli florets
  • 15g (1 Tbsp) olive oil

Cheddar Sauce

  • 800g (3⅓ cups) 2% cottage cheese
  • 250g (2 cups) extra sharp cheddar cheese
  • 30g (¼ cup) cheddar powder
  • 10g (1 Tbsp) nutritional yeast
  • 650g (2¾ cups) whole milk To taste: salt and pepper
  • 80g (⅓ cup) lemon juice
  • 5g (1 tsp) baking soda

Pasta

  • 672g (24 oz) pasta of choice

Instructions

  1. Dice yellow onions, mince the garlic and place in the slow cooker with the chicken thighs, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, onion powder, garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika. Stir to coat evenly.
  2. Cover and cook on high for 3–4 hours or low for 4–5 hours until tender.
  3. While the chicken cooks, boil a pot of salted water and blanch broccoli florets for one minute. Transfer to an ice bath, then drain and chop into bite-sized pieces. Spread on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and bake at 450°F (230°C) for 20 min, stirring halfway. Broil for 5–10 min until browned.
  4. For the cheese sauce, combine baking soda with lemon juice to create sodium citrate (an emulsifier to keep cheese sauces smooth). In a blender, add sodium citrate, sharp cheddar cheese, 2% cottage cheese, cheddar powder, nutritional yeast, whole milk, salt, and pepper until smooth.
