Ingredients

Chipotle Brisket

  • 1340g (48 oz) flat cut brisket
  • 200g (7oz can) chipotle peppers
  • 200g (¾ cup) red enchilada sauce
  • 40g (2 Tbsp) honey
  • 15g (1 Tbsp) apple cider vinegar
  • 1Tbsp salt
  • 1Tbsp garlic powder
  • 1Tbsp onion powder
  • 2tsp black pepper

Cornstarch slurry

  • 16g (2 Tbsp) cornstarch
  • 30g (2 Tbsp) cold water

Tortillas & Garnish

  • 12 large tortillas Chopped cilantro

Eggs

  • 600g (2 ½ cups) egg whites
  • 5 whole eggs
  • 350g (1 ½ cups) 2% cottage cheese
  • 6g (2 tsp) corn starch
  • 100g (1 cup) cheddar cheese

Yogurt Sauce

  • 400g (1 ⅔ cups) 0% Greek yogurt
  • 50g (3 Tbsp) hot sauce To taste: salt, garlic powder, pepper

Instructions

  1. Trim the flat cut brisket by removing excess separable fat (reference trimming guide if needed). Once trimmed, place the meat in the slow cooker. Add the chipotles, red enchilada sauce, honey, apple cider vinegar, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper. mix to coat. Cover and cook on high for 4–5 hours or low for
  2. Remove the meat from the slow cooker. Transfer the liquid to a pan and boil over medium-high heat for 10–15 min to reduce. Stir in the cornstarch and cold water slurry - cook until thickened. Shred the beef with two forks, return it to the pot with the thickened sauce, and mix.
  3. Pre-heat oven to 375 degrees (190°C). In a blender, combine egg whites, whole eggs, 2% cottage cheese, and corn starch. Blend until smooth and pour the mixture into a sheet pan. Shred the cheddar cheese, top the mixture, and bake for 20–30 minutes, or until the middle is firm. Let the baked eggs sit for 10 minutes, then slice
  4. Mix 0% Greek yogurt, hot sauce, salt, garlic powder, and pepper in a small bowl to make the sauce.
  5. To assemble, lay 12 tortillas flat, add a dollop of sauce, a rectangle of baked egg, and a portion of the beef. Garnish with chopped cilantro, wrap the burrito tightly. Serve and store leftovers in foil or a freezer-safe
